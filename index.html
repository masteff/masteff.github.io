What I do

engineering

After school I actually wanted to get into the movie business, so I started an internship at a production company for tv documenteries. But a short time in I realised, that I was much more interested in understanding the internal construction of a camera rather than filming with it. I quit the internship and began to study optical technologies. After my diploma I even continued to advance my knowledge with a master's degree in applied physics. By now I am an engineer by heart and have the strong belief that there is now problem in the world that can't be fixed by a bit of thinking and a lot of trial and error. But jokes aside, ever since my studies I continously improved my engeneering skills in many different fieldd. So although I might not have an expert answer to every question, at least I will probalbly have heard about a topic somewhere before and know what I have to look up.

programming

As with engineering I didnt't quite find my love for programming right away. In fact I wasn't even a big fan of the programming courses in my engeneering classes. Back in the days programming wasn't as popular and I would find it quite boring spending hours to develop a java program that could solve a binomial equations. This changed when I was working on my master thesis where I wanted to find the blood concentration in a humans body by spectoscopy. I had to write a program that controls the spektometer, read out ccd and does all kinds of data manipulation. So this was when I first realised what crazy stuff you can do with programming. Ever since I'm hooked and suck in everything in can find about programming. Having basic knowledge in almost every program language there is I find myself most interested in embedded systems which combines my interst for programming an d electrical engeneering.

automating

This is worth a new column for me although one could argue that automation is just the combination of the first two colums. For me though the urge to automate things almost became like a character trait of me. Don't get me wrong. If I am in a hurry and need quick solutions I would also just copy/paste four blocks of text into an essay. But while doing so I would constantly think about a way to do this more efficiently. I think trying to automate a process as much as possible really lets you dig deep and understand the underlying concepts. As well as eleminating any kind of errors human or system wise. But of course I know that automating a lot and trusting to much in machines or code can also cause a lot of trouble. So I am well aware that every system of machines and programming needs strong validation.
