webserver to uart communication

One of the many goals in a production environment is the optimisation of the employees' workload. In highly automated processes the operator might only switch on a machine and then work on a completely different topic until he gets the notification, that the former machine has completed its work or if there was some kind of failure. Often signal lamps or tones are used in this scenario.

But these approaches are very limited in the amount of information provided. Why not give the operator the opportunity to get the full information on a mobile device that can be carried around all the time?

Of course the information channels should be secured and due to company policy its often not allowed to use the company network in the production area. A bluetooth application would limit the number of operators to just one.

A cheap an easy progrmmable solution would be the integration of an ESP32 microcontroller, which has the functionality to set up isolated wifi network with an integrated webserver. The ESP can communicate directly to the machine over a simple protocol like uart and provide the infomration given over a werbpage.

In the project ESP32 webserver to uart I programmed a simple mocking station in c# sharp, and a firmware for the esp32 which serves a web page to anyone and gives updates about the stations progres. Please also check out the short video which shows operation.
