quality and regulatory

The medical device industry has one of the highest standards in quality control there is. Not only must strict regulations for approval be met, poor product quality can endanger human lives and damage a company's reputation. I have been working in the quality team of a medical device company for almost 5 years now. I work in the design transfer of new products, which means I accompany an article from prototype status to mass production.

For this I need to have quite a good understanding of the functionality of the product and the underlying concepts. A basic understanding of physics principles helps a lot. When the requirements are set and the manufactuing processes are starting to be established I begin to work on test methods to ensure the article specifications.

A test method should be fast and solid.For the automation I design production equipment, which is used in testing. To meet al legal requirements this production equipment is then outsorced to external manufacturers. I accompany this process until the final approval.

A big project I am working on right now is the transfer of the inspection processes into a new system of PLC testing. I work together with the engineers at beckhoff, chosse he right equipment and write the software in structured text.
